Visa Applications

How long does the visa application process take?

Processing times vary by country and visa type, typically ranging from 2 weeks to 6 months. We'll provide you with accurate timelines during your consultation and keep you updated throughout the process.

What documents do I need for a visa application?

Required documents vary by visa type and destination country but commonly include passport, photographs, financial statements, employment letters, educational certificates, and supporting documents. We provide a comprehensive checklist tailored to your application.

Can you guarantee my visa will be approved?

While we cannot guarantee approval as the final decision rests with immigration authorities, we maximize your chances of success by ensuring your application is complete, accurate, and professionally presented with all supporting documentation.

What happens if my visa application is refused?

We'll review the refusal reasons, advise you on your options, and help you understand whether to appeal, reapply, or explore alternative pathways. Our goal is to find the best solution for your situation.

Passport Services

Do you help with passport applications and renewals?

Yes, we provide comprehensive passport services including new applications, renewals, replacements for lost/damaged passports, and expedited processing when available.

How long does it take to get a new passport?

Standard passport processing typically takes 4-8 weeks. Expedited services may be available for urgent cases. We'll guide you through the fastest options available.

Work & Study Permits

What is the difference between a work permit and a work visa?

A work visa allows you to enter a country, while a work permit grants you authorization to work there. Some countries issue combined documents, while others require separate applications. We'll clarify the requirements for your destination.

Can I work while studying abroad?

Many countries allow international students to work part-time during studies and full-time during breaks. Regulations vary by country and visa type. We'll explain the work rights associated with your student visa.
